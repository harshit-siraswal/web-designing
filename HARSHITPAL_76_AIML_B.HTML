Exceptional artworks by INDAIN ARTISTS

Learn more about the painting LADY HOLDING A FRUIT by Raja Ravi Varma: LADY HOLDING A FRUIT

  • The painting shows a woman gracefully holding a fruit in her hand.
  • She is depicted in traditional Indian attire (a sari), with jewelry (earrings, nose ring, bangles etc.) that signify elegance and cultural identity.
  • The skin tones, textures of fabric and jewelry are very finely rendered — showing Ravi Varma’s mastery in blending Indian thematic content with European Naturalism / Academic style.

Learn more about the painting HAMSA AND DAMYAWANTI by Raja Ravi Varma: HAMSA AND DAYAWANTI

  • The painting shows Princess Damayanti, seated gracefully, listening intently to a golden swan (hamsa)
  • According to the Mahabharata tale, the swan is a divine messenger who praises King Nala and tells Damayanti about him.
  • Damayanti is portrayed as captivated, blushing, and absorbed, symbolizing love at first hearing.

Learn more about the painting Ahalyaodhar by Raja Ravi Varma: Ahalyaodhar

  • The painting depicts the story of Ahalya, a character from the Ramayana, who was turned into stone by sage Gautama’s curse and later liberated by Lord Rama’s touch.
  • It emphasizes divine intervention, redemption, and virtue, capturing the moment of Ahalya’s liberation.
  • Often shows Ahalya in a graceful, pleading posture, with sage Gautama and sometimes Lord Rama present. The scene conveys spiritual drama and emotion.

Learn more about the painting DUSHYANTA AND SHAKUNTALA by Raja Ravi Verma:

  • The painting usually shows Dushyanta and Shakuntala in a forest or hermitage setting, highlighting their love story.
  • Based on the story from the Mahabharata and Kalidasa’s play Abhijnanasakuntalam.
  • Famous versions by Raja Ravi Varma show detailed costumes, jewelry, and lifelike human figures.
  • Rich natural backgrounds like forests or rivers, with expressions showing love, longing, or surprise.

Leann more about the painting WOMEN WITH A LAMP by Sawlaram Haldankar

  • Painted by Sawlaram Haldankar in 1945–46 using watercolor.
  • Features Haldankar’s daughter holding a lamp, symbolizing hope.
  • Displayed in Mysore’s Jaganmohan Palace with special lighting to highlight details.
  • Often misattributed to Raja Ravi Varma, celebrated for its delicate realism.
